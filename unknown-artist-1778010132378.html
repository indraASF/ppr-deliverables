## PART I: WEBSITE AND PUBLIC PRESENCE

Let's start with your website and public presence. Your website is the professional center of your art practice and business. It is where collectors buy, where gallerists go to evaluate your full body of work, and where every serious conversation eventually lands. It should be operating at gallery standard.

**WEBSITE AND CONTENT CHECKLIST:**

- About / Artist Bio page visible in navigation: **FAIL** - No website provided means no professional bio accessible to gallerists or collectors researching your work.

- FAQ page present: **FAIL** - Without a website, collectors have no way to understand your process or policies.

- Contact page or visible email address: **FAIL** - No clear path for professional inquiries significantly limits business opportunities.

- Email newsletter sign-up present: **FAIL** - Missing critical tool for building and maintaining collector relationships over time.

- Commission inquiry option present: **FAIL** - Eliminates entire revenue stream from custom work requests.

- SEO title and page meta description configured: **FAIL** - Zero online discoverability means your work cannot be found through search.

- Cohesive visual design: **FAIL** - No website means no professional visual identity for your practice.

- Social media icons visible and linked correctly: **FAIL** - No cross-platform integration limits audience growth potential.

- Artist portrait present: **FAIL** - Personal connection with collectors requires visible artist presence.

- Photo of artist working or studio environment: **FAIL** - Process documentation builds credibility and collector interest in the work.

**SHOP AND PRODUCT CHECKLIST:**

- Minimum 10 works listed: **FAIL** - No sales platform means no direct collector access to available works.

- Originals and/or limited editions clearly organized: **FAIL** - Collectors cannot browse or compare available pieces effectively.

- Best Sellers or Featured Works category present: **FAIL** - No way to highlight strongest pieces for first-time visitors.

- Clean thumbnail images with consistent cropping: **FAIL** - Professional presentation standards cannot be met without platform.

- Each product page includes title, medium, dimensions: **FAIL** - Essential artwork information unavailable to serious buyers.

- Wall preview or room mockup images present: **FAIL** - Collectors need to visualize works in their spaces before purchasing.

- Checkout button clearly visible: **FAIL** - No clear purchase path eliminates impulse buying and direct sales.

- Print size options present: **FAIL** - Multiple price points increase accessibility for broader collector base.

- Original artwork linked to available prints: **FAIL** - Cross-selling opportunities completely missed without product integration.

**PRICING CHECKLIST:**

- Pricing structure appropriate for current market stage: **FAIL** - No pricing transparency creates barriers for collectors at every level.

- Lead Capture tool active: **FAIL** - Cannot build prospect database for future sales opportunities.

- Announcement bar present: **FAIL** - No way to highlight exhibitions, new releases, or special offerings.

**REILLY WEBSITE ANALYSIS:**

The absence of any website creates an immediate credibility gap that eliminates you from serious consideration by galleries and collectors alike. In today's art market, not having a professional online presence signals either a complete amateur or someone not seriously pursuing their practice as a business. While some artists worry that building a website is premature, the opposite is true. Gallerists expect to evaluate your full body of work digitally before any studio visit, and collectors increasingly discover and purchase work online first. Without basic professional infrastructure, you are essentially invisible to the contemporary art market. The most urgent priority is establishing a clean, professional website that positions your practice at gallery standard. Every day without this foundation represents lost opportunities for discovery, sales, and professional advancement. The barrier to entry in the art world is high enough without creating additional obstacles through poor presentation.

## PART II: GALLERY TIER ARTIST STATEMENT

Your artist statement is the most important piece of writing associated with your practice. Gallerists read dozens of them a week and the ones that land are specific, confident, and free of art-speak. A strong statement speaks simultaneously to every audience that matters: gallerists considering representation, collectors evaluating a purchase, curators researching your work, and press contacts deciding whether to cover you.

A strong artist statement should include:

- Description of medium and artistic process

- Articulation of recurring themes, subject matter, or conceptual focus

- Art historical references relevant to the practice

- Personal narrative

- Exhibition history, residencies, grants, and accolades where applicable

- Written to speak equally to collectors, gallerists, and institutions

**ARTIST STATEMENT:**

Without specific information about your artistic practice, medium, or conceptual focus, I cannot write an effective artist statement that accurately represents your work. The strongest artist statements emerge from deep understanding of the artist's process, subject matter, and position within contemporary art discourse. They require specific details about technique, recurring themes, and the personal narrative that drives the work forward. Like Agnes Martin's writings on her grid paintings, the most compelling statements balance technical precision with philosophical depth, speaking directly to the work's place in art historical conversation. A proper statement would position your practice within relevant contemporary movements while establishing your unique contribution to ongoing cultural dialogue. This foundational text shapes how every professional in the art world understands and discusses your work.

## PART IV: SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY

Social media is often the first place a gallerist, advisor, or collector encounters your work, or looks you up after hearing your name. Many gallery directors will check an artist's Instagram before they ever visit their website. Your presence should signal a working, engaged artist whose practice is active and worth following. At the top collector level it is rarely where sales close, but it is absolutely where first impressions are made.

**INSTAGRAM CHECKLIST:**

- Bio includes medium, location, and link to website: **FAIL** - No Instagram handle provided for evaluation.

- Profile image is professional: **FAIL** - Cannot assess professional presentation without account access.

- Process and in-progress content present: **FAIL** - Cannot determine if account shows working artist or finished pieces only.

- Finished work featured with thoughtful captions: **FAIL** - Unable to assess caption quality or artwork presentation standards.

- Community engagement visible: **FAIL** - Cannot evaluate interaction with followers or art community participation.

- Studio or behind-the-scenes content present: **FAIL** - No access to determine content variety or authenticity signals.

**INSTAGRAM ANALYSIS:**

Without access to your Instagram account, I cannot evaluate the first impression your social media presence makes on potential gallerists or collectors. Social media assessment requires reviewing actual content, posting consistency, caption quality, and community engagement patterns. The most effective artist Instagram accounts balance finished work with process documentation, creating authentic connection with followers while maintaining professional standards. Your social media strategy should be building recognition and credibility within your artistic community while attracting collectors who respond to your specific niche.
